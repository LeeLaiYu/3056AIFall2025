# Lab 2: Letter to the Editor Draft

## Overview
This lab helps you learn to write effective letters to the editor by analyzing published examples and using AI to generate a draft that matches the publication's tone and style.

## Objectives
- Analyze sample letters to the editor and editorials to understand tone and style
- Create a structured outline for your letter
- Use AI to generate a letter draft that matches publication standards
- Learn to work within word count limits and style requirements

## Prerequisites
- Completion of Lab 0 and Lab 1
- Access to SCMP Letters section (https://www.scmp.com/comment/letters)
- Basic understanding of argumentative writing
- Access to an AI coding assistant (like Cursor)

## Instructions
See [INSTRUCTIONS.md](./INSTRUCTIONS.md) for detailed step-by-step instructions.

## Workflow

1. **Gather Samples** - Collect 3-5 letters to the editor and/or editorials from SCMP
2. **Analyze Style** - Review the samples to understand tone, style, and structure
3. **Create Outline** - Write your letter outline with key points and arguments
4. **Use AI** - Ask AI to generate a letter draft based on samples and outline
5. **Review & Refine** - Check the generated draft and refine as needed

## Key Deliverables

1. **Input Files** (in `input/` folder)
   - Sample letters/editorials from SCMP (3-5 files)
   - Letter outline with key points and style preferences

2. **Output File** (in `output/` folder, generated by AI)
   - `letter_draft.md` - Letter draft matching SCMP style

## Resources
- SCMP Letters: https://www.scmp.com/comment/letters
- Template for outline: `input/letter_outline_template.md`

## Notes
- Gather diverse samples to understand different styles and topics
- Be specific in your outline about tone preferences (formal, persuasive, critical, etc.)
- Ensure your letter addresses a current issue or responds to recent coverage
- Check word count limits for SCMP Letters section
- Review and edit the AI-generated draft to ensure accuracy and personal voice
