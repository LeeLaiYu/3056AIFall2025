# SCMP Letter to the Editor: Energy Subsidies Miss the Mark
## Target: 200-250 words | Focus: Universal vs. Targeted Approach

---

## OPENING HOOK (25-30 words)
"Hong Kong's electricity subsidy gives HK$1,200 to every household, but the most vulnerable residents often see no benefit at all."

## CONTEXT SETTING (40-50 words)
- Energy poverty: inability to afford adequate energy services
- Government's "targeted poverty alleviation" strategy vs. universal ECRS approach
- Rising energy costs and climate challenges

## CORE PROBLEM: SUBDIVIDED UNIT GAP (60-70 words)
**The Landlord Interception Issue:**
- ECRS credits go to landlord's master meter account
- No legal requirement for landlords to pass savings to tenants
- Most vulnerable populations (subdivided unit tenants) receive zero benefit
- Government acknowledges this gap but provides no solution
- **Data point:** Census & Statistics confirmed energy expenditure by subdivided units "not available"

## WASTEFUL RESOURCE DISTRIBUTION (50-60 words)
**Universal Approach Problems:**
- Same HK$1,200 subsidy for luxury apartments and subdivided units
- High-income households don't need assistance
- Contradicts government's own "targeted poverty alleviation" policy
- Administrative costs for universal distribution vs. targeted approach
- **Data point:** 23.6% pre-intervention poverty rate, but universal subsidies don't target the poor

## MISSED OPPORTUNITIES (40-50 words)
**Sustainability Gap:**
- No incentives for energy-efficient appliances
- MEELS labeling exists but no financial support for upgrades
- Poor households stuck with inefficient, expensive-to-run appliances
- Missed opportunity to combine poverty relief with green goals

## PROPOSED SOLUTION (30-40 words)
- Replace universal subsidies with income-tested, tiered system
- Direct tenant support for subdivided units
- Rebates for energy-efficient appliance purchases
- Align with government's stated "targeted" approach

## CALL TO ACTION (20-25 words)
"Hong Kong needs energy policies that actually help the poor while promoting sustainability. The current approach achieves neither."

## REFINED VERSION (Target: 200-250 words)

### OPENING (25 words)
"Hong Kong's electricity subsidy gives HK$1,200 to every household, but the most vulnerable residents often see no benefit at all."

### CONTEXT (35 words)
Energy poverty affects those unable to afford adequate energy services. While the government promotes "targeted poverty alleviation," the Energy Charges Relief Scheme (ECRS) remains universal and ineffective.

### CORE PROBLEM (55 words)
The scheme fails subdivided unit tenants completely. ECRS credits go to landlords' master meter accounts with no legal requirement to pass savings to tenants. The Census Department confirms energy expenditure data for subdivided units is "not available," highlighting this policy blind spot.

### WASTE & OPPORTUNITY (50 words)
The universal approach wastes resources on wealthy households while missing sustainability opportunities. No incentives exist for energy-efficient appliances, leaving poor households with expensive-to-run, inefficient equipment despite the MEELS labeling scheme.

### SOLUTION (30 words)
Replace universal subsidies with income-tested support, direct tenant payments for subdivided units, and rebates for energy-efficient appliances. This aligns with government's "targeted poverty alleviation" strategy.

### CALL TO ACTION (20 words)
"Hong Kong needs energy policies that actually help the poor while promoting sustainability. The current approach achieves neither."

**TOTAL: ~215 words**
